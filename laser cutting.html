‚Ä¢ What is laser cutting?

Today I was introduced to the fascinating world of laser cutting technology. I learned how concentrated light beams can precisely cut through various materials with incredible accuracy. The different laser types each have their strengths - CO2 lasers excel at organic materials like wood and acrylic, while fiber lasers are perfect for metals. Understanding safety protocols was crucial, as laser cutting involves high-powered focused energy that requires proper protective equipment and ventilation systems.

Day 2 ‚Äì Hardware & Software

Learning Objectives:
‚Ä¢ Laser cutter parts (laser tube, bed, exhaust, etc.)
‚Ä¢ Software: LaserCAD (version)
‚Ä¢ Vector vs Raster, file types (.svg, .dxf)

Day two focused on understanding the machine components and software workflow. I learned about the laser tube that generates the beam, the cutting bed where materials are placed, the exhaust system for fume extraction, and the motion control systems. The distinction between vector cutting (for outlines) and raster engraving (for images) became clear, along with proper file formats like SVG for vectors and how to prepare designs in LaserCAD software.

Day 3 ‚Äì Design Basics

Learning Objectives:
‚Ä¢ Cut vs engrave setup
‚Ä¢ Design tips for press fit

The third day delved into practical design considerations that make the difference between failed and successful projects. I learned how cutting operations require different power and speed settings compared to engraving, and how to set up files accordingly. Press-fit joint design became a key skill - understanding how to account for material thickness and kerf width to create tight-fitting assemblies without adhesives.

Day 4 ‚Äì Materials & Settings

Learning Objectives:
‚Ä¢ Materials: wood, acrylic (types), paper, cardboard etc.
‚Ä¢ Power/speed settings
‚Ä¢ Testing cuts

Day four was all about understanding the relationship between materials and machine settings. I discovered how different materials require specific combinations of laser power, cutting speed, and multiple passes. Wood grain direction affects cutting quality, acrylic types (cast vs extruded) behave differently, and paper requires very low power to avoid burning. The importance of test cuts became apparent - always run small samples before committing to full projects.

Day 5 ‚Äì Operating the Laser

Learning Objectives:
‚Ä¢ Focusing, bed setup, air assist, exhaust
‚Ä¢ Sending files, running job
‚Ä¢ Safety checks (emergency stop)

The excitement of day five came with hands-on machine operation! I learned the critical process of laser focusing - adjusting the distance between the lens and material surface for optimal cut quality. Bed setup involves securing materials flat and level, while air assist helps blow away debris and prevent flare-ups. Understanding the complete workflow from file transfer to job completion, including constant safety monitoring and knowing where the emergency stop is located.

‚Ä¢ File prep, nesting, assembly

Project day brought together all previous learning into creating a functional object! I planned and executed a complete project from concept to finished assembly. Whether creating a phone stand, storage box, or 3D puzzle, this day emphasized the complete design-to-fabrication workflow that makes laser cutting so powerful for rapid prototyping.

The final day focused on troubleshooting skills and machine care that ensure consistent results. The day concluded with presenting final projects and reflecting on the complete learning journey from safety basics to independent operation.
